Source

Reference data comes from EnergyPlus TMYx calculated undisturbed ground temperatures at 2.0 m depth for: AUS_QLD_Rockhampton.AP.943740_TMYx.stat, AUS_NT_Alice.Springs.AP.943260_TMYx.stat, AUS_NSW_Mascot.AP-Sydney.Intl.AP.947670_TMYx.stat, AUS_VIC_Tullamarine-Melbourne.Intl.AP.948660_TMYx.stat, and AUS_ACT_Canberra.Intl.AP.949260_TMYx.stat.

These are calculated ground temperatures, not one measured calendar year.

Model weather comes from PVGIS TMY via the local backend. A TMY is a representative year, not one specific year.

Reference data: EnergyPlus TMYx calculated undisturbed ground temperatures at 2.0m depth, sourced from climate.onebuilding.org. Ground temperatures calculated using standard soil diffusivity of 2.3225760E-03 m²/day.

Source link: https://climate.onebuilding.org/WMO_Region_5_Southwest_Pacific/AUS_Australia/index.html

Findings
The model gets the seasonal shape right, but it stays too warm and does not drop enough in winter. This suggests the Burch-Christensen settings were tuned for US conditions and are too warm for Australian sites.
1. Elevation
Canberra airport is at 575 m above sea level, much higher than Sydney (6 m), Rockhampton (10 m), and Melbourne (132 m). Higher elevation means colder ground temperatures, but the model only uses air temperature and does not account for altitude.
