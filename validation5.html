Validation5: Mains Water Temperature Formula

algorithm used in the calculator to estimate mains water temperature from ambient temperature data.

Main Formula

T_mains,F = (T_avg,F + 6) + ratio * (DeltaT_month,F / 2) * sin(angle)
The +6 is a Fahrenheit offset that accounts for the ground being warmer than the air on average.
in code
const tMainsF = (annualAvgTaF + 6) + ratio * (deltaMonthF / 2) * Math.sin(angleRad);

Algorithm Calculation

ratio = 0.4 + 0.01 * (T_avg,F - 44)
lag = 35 - (T_avg,F - 44)
angle = 0.986 * (modelDay - 15 - lag) - 90
Converts ambient temperature inputs to Fahrenheit, then uses the sinusoidal formula to estimate daily mains temperature.

What Each Term Means

TermMeaning
T_mains,FEstimated mains water temperature in Fahrenheit
T_avg,FAnnual average ambient air temperature in Fahrenheit
DeltaT_month,FDifference between the warmest and coolest monthly average ambient temperatures in Fahrenheit
ratioControls how large the seasonal swing is
lagShifts the timing of the sinusoidal curve through the year
modelDayDay number used by the sinusoidal model, adjusted for hemisphere
angleAngle fed into the sine function for the daily temperature estimate

How The Calculator Uses It

1. Load TMY weather data.
2. Calculate monthly average ambient temperature.
3. Find the annual average ambient temperature and the monthly temperature range.
4. Use the sinusoidal formula to estimate daily mains water temperature.
5. Average those daily values into monthly mains temperatures for display and modelling.
